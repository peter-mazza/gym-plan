PETER'S GYM PLAN

4-day split · Southrac · Updated March 2026

DAY 1 — PUSH
Chest · Shoulders · Triceps · Core
⚠️ Only 3 days this week? Drop Day 4, keep Days 1–3.
Warm-Up
5 min Incline Ramp (or substitute)
Zone 1 · HR 100–120 · Easy conversational pace
Main Work
Incline Dumbbell Press 3×10

Start weight: Try 25–35 lb dumbbells. Last 2 reps should feel genuinely hard without losing form.
Form tip: Keep shoulder blades pinched back into the bench. Don't let elbows flare past 45°.

▶ Watch demo
Seated DB Shoulder Press 3×10

Start weight: 20–30 lb dumbbells.
Form tip: Press in a slight forward arc, not straight up. Stop just before lockout to keep tension on the muscle.

▶ Watch demo
Cable Lateral Raise 3×12

Start weight: 5–10 lbs on the cable stack.
Form tip: Stand sideways to the cable. Lead with your elbow, not your wrist. Don't shrug at the top.

▶ Watch demo
Chest Fly Machine 3×12

Start weight: 40–60 lbs on the stack.
Form tip: Think about hugging a tree, not pushing. Full stretch at the open position, controlled close.

▶ Watch demo
Tricep Rope Pushdown 3×12

Start weight: 30–40 lbs.
Form tip: At the bottom, spread the rope handles apart to fully contract the tricep. Keep elbows pinned to your sides.

▶ Watch demo
Overhead Tricep Extension (cable) 2×12

Start weight: 20–30 lbs.
Form tip: Face away from the cable, rope attachment at the top. Keep elbows close to your head. Full stretch down, squeeze up.

▶ Watch demo
Core
Plank 3×40 sec

Form tip: Squeeze glutes and abs simultaneously. Don't let hips sag or pike. Breathe steadily. If 40 sec is too easy, move to RKC plank variation.

▶ Watch demo
Dead Bug 3×10 each side

Form tip: Press your lower back into the floor throughout — this is the whole point. Lower opposite arm and leg simultaneously, slow and controlled. Don't rush.

DAY 2 — PULL
Back · Biceps · Rear Delts · Core
Warm-Up
5 min Incline Ramp (or substitute)
Zone 1 · HR 100–120
Main Work
Seated Cable Row (V-bar, narrow) 3×10

Grip: Use the V-bar (neutral/narrow grip). This gives fuller range of motion and is easier on the shoulders.
Start weight: 50–70 lbs.
Form tip: Sit tall, pull the handle to your lower sternum, squeeze shoulder blades together at the end. Don't lean back excessively — slight lean only.

▶ Watch demo
Lat Pulldown (wide grip) 3×10

Start weight: 60–80 lbs.
Form tip: Lean back slightly, pull bar to upper chest, lead with your elbows. Don't jerk or use momentum.

▶ Watch demo
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row 3×10 each

Start weight: 30–40 lbs.
Form tip: Brace with your opposite hand on the bench. Pull the elbow straight back past your hip. Think "elbow to ceiling," not "hand to hip."

▶ Watch demo
Face Pulls (cable) 3×15

Don't skip this one. It's the best shoulder health exercise available.
Start weight: 20–30 lbs. Keep it light — this is not a strength exercise.
Form tip: Set cable at face height, rope attachment. Pull to your ears, end position has elbows high and wrists behind elbows. Slow and controlled.

▶ Watch demo
EZ Bar Curl 3×12

Start weight: 40–55 lbs on the EZ bar.
Form tip: Keep elbows pinned at your sides. Full stretch at the bottom, squeeze at the top. The angled grip is easier on wrists than a straight bar.

▶ Watch demo
Hammer Curl 2×12

Start weight: 20–30 lbs.
Form tip: Palms facing each other, curl straight up. Hits the brachialis (under the bicep) and forearm — adds thickness and grip strength.

▶ Watch demo
Core
Bird Dog 3×10 each side

Form tip: On all fours, extend opposite arm and leg simultaneously. Keep hips level — no rotation. Pause 1 second at full extension. This is a back rehab staple.

▶ Watch demo
🔥 CARDIO FINISHER
10–15 min · Zone 3 · HR 135–155
Push the incline or pace. You want to be breathing hard. This is where the endorphins are.
DAY 3 — LEGS + GLUTES
Most important day for back health & testosterone
Glute Activation (band)
Do these BEFORE loading weight
Clamshells — 2×15 each side
Glute Bridge with band — 2×15
This fires up the glutes so they actually do the work during the heavy lifts instead of your lower back compensating.
Warm-Up
5 min Incline Ramp
Zone 1–2 · HR 115–135 · Moderate pace
Main Work
Goblet Squat 3×12

Start weight: 25–35 lb dumbbell.
Form tip: Hold dumbbell vertically at your chest. Squat deep, elbows inside knees at the bottom. Great for reinforcing good squat mechanics with lower back stress.

▶ Watch demo
Single-Leg RDL (kettlebell) 3×10 each

Start weight: 20–30 lb kettlebell (contralateral hand — opposite to planted foot).
Form tip: Hinge at the hip, flat back, free leg extends behind as counterbalance. Your goal of touching near the ankle is a good depth reference, but stop short if your lower back rounds — flat back is more important than depth. Slow and controlled beats fast every time.

▶ Watch demo
Hip Thrust (barbell or machine) 3×12

Start weight: Bodyweight or 45 lb bar to start.
Form tip: Upper back on the bench, feet flat and close to glutes. Drive through your heels, squeeze glutes hard at the top. Don't hyperextend the low back at the top — ribs down, chin tucked.

▶ Watch demo
Leg Press 3×12

Start weight: 90–135 lbs on the sled.
Foot placement: Lower and wider on the platform = more glute and hamstring. Higher = more quad.
Form tip: Don't lock out knees at the top. Lower the weight until hips start to tuck off the pad — stop there.

▶ Watch demo
Walking Lunges 3×10 each leg

Start weight: Bodyweight or 15–20 lb dumbbells.
Form tip: Big step forward, back knee drops toward (not to) the floor. Keep torso upright. Use the turf area at Southrac if available.

▶ Watch demo
Leg Curl Machine 3×12

Start weight: 40–60 lbs.
Form tip: Slow on the way down (eccentric). Don't let hips lift off the pad when curling. Full range of motion.

▶ Watch demo
Core
Ab Wheel 3×10

Form tip: Start from kneeling. Keep core tight and don't let your lower back sag as you roll out. Only go as far as you can control. This is harder than it looks.

▶ Watch demo
Side Plank 2×30 sec each side

Form tip: Stack feet or stagger them. Drive your hip up — don't let it sag. This trains lateral core stability, critical for back health.

▶ Watch demo

Your legs will be cooked — the ramp at a moderate incline at a strong pace works great here. Stair climber is also a good option after leg day.
DAY 4 — UPPER + CONDITIONING
No legs today — they need the recovery after Day 3
⚠️ Only 3 days this week? This is the day to drop. Days 1–3 cover everything.
Warm-Up
5 min Incline Ramp
Zone 1 · HR 100–120
Main Work
Dumbbell Bench Press 3×10

Start weight: 30–40 lbs each dumbbell.
Form tip: Flat bench. This is horizontal pressing to balance Day 1's incline work. Lower the dumbbells until they're at chest level, press straight up. Keep shoulder blades retracted.

▶ Watch demo
Cable Pull-Through 3×15

Replaces kettlebell swing — same pattern, much easier to learn.
Start weight: 30–40 lbs.
Form tip: Low cable, rope attachment, face away from the machine. Hinge at the hips (not the waist), let the rope swing between your legs, drive hips forward to stand. All the power comes from the glute squeeze at the top. Back stays flat throughout.

▶ Watch demo
Standard Push-Up 3×12

Form tip: Body in a straight line, core tight. Lower until chest nearly touches the floor. If 12 reps is too easy, elevate feet on a bench for more challenge.

▶ Watch demo
Seated Cable Row (V-bar, narrow) 3×10

Same as Day 2. See notes there for form cues.

▶ Watch demo
Arnold Press 3×10

Start weight: 20–25 lbs each dumbbell.
Form tip: Start with palms facing you (like a curl finish), rotate palms outward as you press up. Hits all three heads of the shoulder. Named after Arnold Schwarzenegger.

▶ Watch demo
Face Pulls 2×15

Repeated from Day 2 intentionally — shoulder health investment. See Day 2 for form cues.

Core Circuit — 2 Rounds
Plank → Dead Bug → Bird Dog 2 rounds

Plank: 40 sec
Dead Bug: 10 each side
Bird Dog: 10 each side

Rest 30–45 sec between rounds. Full form cues on each exercise in Day 1 (Plank, Dead Bug) and Day 2 (Bird Dog).

10–15 min · Zone 3 · HR 135–155
Push it. This is your conditioning day — the finisher matters more here than any other day.

When you don't want the incline ramp
Heart Rate Zones
Zone 1 · 95–115 BPM · Warm-up / Day 5 cardio
Zone 2 · 115–135 BPM · Fat burn / aerobic base
Zone 3 · 135–155 BPM · Target for finishers · Endorphin zone
Zone 4 · 155–170 BPM · Occasional use only for now
Incline Ramp Alternatives
🏃 Indoor Track (jog)
Southrac has a 1/8-mile indoor track. Easy to hit Zone 3 with a moderate jog. Low impact, no learning curve.
🚴 Stationary Bike
Very back-friendly. Reliable Zone 3 at moderate resistance and cadence. Best option if your back is flaring.
🪜 Stair Climber
High endorphin output. Glute-heavy — great after leg days. Hits Zone 3 fast.
🚣 Rowing Machine
Full body, excellent conditioning. Learn the form before going hard — poor rowing form can stress your lower back. Strong option once comfortable.

Priority Order
1. Hit protein first — if you miss one macro, don't let it be this one
2. Hit total calories — being significantly under will slow recovery
3. Fill in carbs — especially pre-workout
4. Let fat flex — you're already fat-heavy by habit, so this can adjust
Protein Strategy
Whey protein: 2 scoops/day = ~40–50g with zero meal planning.
1 scoop post-workout · 1 scoop at another meal or in a smoothie.

Drinking Days (3x/week)
~3 drinks ≈ 300–400 kcal. On those days:
Drop 100–150g of carbs, not fat.
Keep protein high regardless. Your body burns alcohol first, so lower carbs = less fat storage risk.
